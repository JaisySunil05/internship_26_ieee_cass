# 3. Synthesis

## Overview

Synthesis is the process of transforming the RTL (Register Transfer Level) design into a gate-level netlist while satisfying the specified timing and design constraints. This stage was carried out using **Cadence Genus**, where the Verilog design was synthesized and optimized for area, timing, and power.

---

## Objectives

- Convert RTL design into a gate-level netlist.
- Apply timing constraints using SDC.
- Optimize the design for timing, area, and power.
- Generate synthesis reports for analysis.

---

## Tools Used

- Cadence Genus
- Verilog HDL
- Standard Cell Library

---

## Synthesis Flow

1. Read the RTL Verilog files.
2. Load the technology library.
3. Apply design constraints.
4. Perform logic synthesis and optimization.
5. Generate the synthesized gate-level netlist.
6. Analyze timing, area, and power reports.

---

## Learning Outcomes

- Understood the RTL-to-gate-level synthesis process.
- Learned to apply timing constraints using SDC.
- Gained hands-on experience with Cadence Genus.
- Analyzed synthesis reports to evaluate design quality.
